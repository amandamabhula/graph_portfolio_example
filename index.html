About me

I’m a curious, impact‑oriented scientist who enjoys turning messy questions into structured experiments and clear stories. At Duke University Medical Center, I contributed to a project investigating how the localization of a central innate immune adaptor (Protein Y) affects antiviral responses—optimizing protocols across qRT‑PCR, luciferase assays, microscopy, and more. I compiled findings in STATA and shared updates in lab meetings and journal clubs.

Outside the lab, I serve my community through teaching and language support—from SAT tutoring to volunteering with GANO to help Spanish‑speaking families build English proficiency. These experiences shaped my interest in accessible science and public‑facing communication.

About the course

Generative AI for Work & Research is an 8‑week, live, online program focused on building end‑to‑end AI workflows—no coding experience required. Over weekly workshops and help sessions, learners automate document and data tasks, organize knowledge, build custom chatbots, and design portfolio‑ready projects.
